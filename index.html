Crowdsource digital tasks to a community you create

OpenGuilds is a platform to build crowdsourced organizations that can scale to thousands of individuals.

Crowdsource a Workforce of Your Control

Guilds are created with a transparent constitution of your creation.

Guilds are established with a set of transparent rules, written into a guild's constitution. The constitution outlines how workers are onboarded or promoted, how rewards are distributed, and how the rules themselves can be changed.

Skilled workers join guilds, gain qualifications, and are rewarded for their work.

Guilds are only limited by the number of members they can attract. No more overhead, members are rewarded directly based on the merit of their work done. Guilds can process one task a day, or thousands, the only limit is the guild's total membership.

Guilds Have Layers

Guilds support advanced workflows by pushing work done through layers. A layer defines how the work in a previous layer should be transformed and output to the next. In this way simple tasks done by different workers can be combined to give accurate results.

Workers can be promoted automatically through to more trusted layers by completing tasks correctly according to the guild's constitution. Deeper layers are more subjective and can be used to combine or enhance submitted work from previous workers.

Open Guilds Work

Imagine a large outer circle and smaller inner circle. Most work done by guilds such as image labelling, are easy and verifiable. They are done as much as possible by the outer circle, consisting of crowdsourced workers from outside your organization. When a subjective or expert opinion is needed, or workers disagree on an answer, the work can be passed to the more trusted inner circle for verification. This is a simple example of how Open Guilds provide quality control.

Workers Are Rewarded

A guild multiplies its productivity by promoting good workers through its layers for greater rewards and more trusted work. This allows for the scalability that makes crowdsourcing attractive.

Workers can be members of multiple guilds and gain qualifications for many different kinds of digital work. They are not dependent on a single guild for their livelihood in the same way a guild is not dependent on any single worker.

Use Open Guilds to Scale the Unscalable

See how different groups can use OpenGuilds to solve big challenges:

Non-Profits

Non-profits can pair refugees from all over the world with documents that need to be translated. Help people learn their native language and check each others work while earning a living

Government Organizations and NGO's

Governments can use a guilds network during times of crisis to identify issues on the ground in areas that still have mobile networks. NGO's are able to get valuable data from tougher economic zones where cellphone penetration but lacks other infrastructure for effective surveying.

Businesses

Businesses can use a guild to handle normally difficult to scale processes as they grow without increasing overhead. Tasks such as directing customer questions to the appropriate knowledge repository, or labelling valuable data that needs human intervention.

Entrepreneurs

Entrepreneurs can create flexible organizations which are competitive with large competitors without the overhead that requires upfront capital investment. The people needed can be added exactly with the incomming demand, creating a automatic scaling of new business processes.

Everyone

Everyone can use a guild to perform meaningful work online with others. Even those outside of the normal economic opportunity can do work and provide for themselves with nothing but a cellphone and internet connection.
